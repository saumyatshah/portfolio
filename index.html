Identity

// who.is

I work at the boundary between hardware and system — where Verilog meets timing closure, where a Gerber file meets a production line, where firmware meets a shipping product. My range covers the full digital stack: from RTL through GDSII, from FPGA fabric to embedded firmware, from a customer's CAD output to a populated PCB on its way out the door.

Across nine engineering projects and three roles, I've designed and verified a 7nm stereo audio ASIC through the full Synopsys flow, accelerated Sobel edge-detection on FPGA with ARM HPS co-design (14% latency cut via HLS), built a custom 8-bit RISC processor and FPU with pipelining and hazard detection on GF65 cells, hardened fault coverage by 10–12% through Synopsys DC + TetraMAX ATPG, analyzed branch predictor and cache strategies in GEM5, shipped an ESP32-S3 IoT firmware platform with FreeRTOS + MQTT + OTA + BLE Wi-Fi provisioning, ran multi-sensor acquisition on STM32F7, built a 4-wheel holonomic robot on STM32F4 with fuzzy-logic navigation, and laid out custom KiCAD PCBs for the embedded systems they sit on.

Today, I'm a Development Engineer at Express Fab USA, building the internal tooling that makes customer PCB designs production-ready — a Python Gerber automation engine that adds panelization rails with curved trapezoidal profiles and native-geometry barcodes, robust multi-CAD file parsers, and the company's Automated Optical Inspection (AOI) capability built from the ground up, with CAD-driven program generation, line setup, and result analysis tuned for low false calls. Prior: Embedded Engineer at Winwinlabs (ESP32-S3 firmware, OTA, custom production PCB), and an early R&D internship at Thruster instrumenting an STM32F4-based SRM motor-driver assessment rig. M.S. Electrical Engineering, UT Dallas · B.E. ECE, Nirma University.
